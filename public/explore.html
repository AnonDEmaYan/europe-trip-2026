Tip: Pick a sunset sailing so the tower lights up during dessert; allow ~2–2.5 h on board + arrive 30 min early for boarding. Food is often tourist-tier — you’re paying for the view; pair with a great lunch elsewhere if you want peak gastronomy.

Champagne & nearby (from Paris)

~1 h from Paris: TGV Gare de l’Est → Reims (~45 min). Épernay is the other hub (Avenue de Champagne); Hautvillers is a pretty hill village between them — combine with trains or a small-group tour. Book tastings in advance.

  • Paris Gare de l’Est SNCF / SNCF Connect — TGV & regional east
  • Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Reims Gothic façade — UNESCO, near station
  • Gare de Reims Walkable to cathedral & maisons
  • Champagne Taittinger (Reims) Cellar tours — official booking
  • Veuve Clicquot (Reims) Prestige visits — reserve
  • Ruinart (Reims) Chalk cellars — book well ahead
  • Gare d’Épernay Regional from Reims or direct routes — check same-day
  • Avenue de Champagne, Épernay Moët & Chandon, Perrier-Jouët, etc.
  • Hautvillers Dom Pérignon’s village — vineyards & abbey; often paired with Épernay/Reims by tour or local bus
  • Épernay ↔ Hautvillers Short hop — verify bus / taxi / tour same day
  • Les Caves du Louvre Sparkling intro in Paris if you skip the region
  • Les Caves de Taillevent Retail + by-the-glass in Paris
