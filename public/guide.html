Foundation

📋 Before you go

  • Delta app: Save boarding passes offline; turn on flight notifications for DL 194 and your BCN → ATL flight (often DL 195 on public schedules).
  • Seats: For long hauls, aisle or window beats middle; if you sleep on planes, a window + neck pillow wins. Check seat maps for recline and proximity to galleys/lavatories.
  • Documents: Passport + a photo backup; ESTA isn’t needed for US citizens — others should verify entry rules.
  • Money: One no-foreign-fee card as primary; small cash (€) for tapas bars and taxis that don’t take cards.
  • Phone: eSIM or roaming plan so you have maps and Delta alerts in Spain and France.

Pro move

Pack a change of shirt + toothbrush in your carry-on. If a bag is delayed on the outbound or return, you’re still comfortable for day one or the ride home from ATL.

How you want to travel

🍽️ Authentic food · healthy · spicy

  • Spain & France aren’t “hot” cuisines by default. For authentic + lighter: lean on grilled fish, vegetable tapas (pimientos, espinacas con garbanzos, escalivada), gazpacho / salmorejo, menú del día with fish + salad, and market counters where you choose plates.
  • Spice nights: Plan Indian, Thai, Mexican, or Sichuan meals (each city has pockets — see Explore). Say “muy picante” / “très épicé” / ask for extra salsa brava on patatas where it fits.
  • Balance: Heavy traditional dinner one night, fish + veg or spicy Asian the next — you stay authentic without only fried tapas.

👟 ~10,000 steps & maximize time

  • Track it: Phone or watch — all three cities reward walking.
  • Stack sightseeing with distance: Do one big morning walk (often 4–6k steps) before lunch, then use metro only for long hops (>25–30 min on foot).
  • Barcelona: Gothic → Born → Ciutadella rim → Barceloneta boardwalk (or reverse) can blow past 10k in a half-day. Bunkers add vertical + views.
  • Madrid: Full Retiro loop + walk La Latina–Sol–Gran Vía axis; add Malasaña blocks in evening.
  • Paris: Seine bridges + Tuileries + Marais grid — easy 10k+ without feeling “exercisey.” Prefer stairs over escalators on short metro changes when you’re not hauling bags.

🛍️ Clothes: quality without breaking the bank

  • Step-up basics: COS, Arket, Uniqlo in EU — better fabrics and cuts than disposable fast fashion at still-reasonable prices.
  • Spain deals: Lefties (Inditex outlet line) + normal Zara/Massimo on sale rails.
  • Outlet villages: La Roca (BCN area), Las Rozas / San Sebastián de los Reyes (Madrid), La Vallée Village (Paris region) — half-day trips, best for known brands at discount.
  • Paris fun cheap: Kilo Shop (vintage by weight), Guerrisol / Emmaüs for thrift. See Explore · Paris lists.

Seine dinner + Eiffel

Book a sunset dinner cruise (Bateaux Parisiens & alternatives on Explore) — budget ~2.5–3 h including boarding. Food is usually about the view, not Michelin; keep lunch as your “foodie” anchor if you want both.

Start & finish

🌊 Barcelona

  • First days (Mar 31–Apr 1): Jet lag favors morning light outdoors — Gothic Quarter or waterfront walk before a late Spanish lunch.
  • Sagrada Família: Book a timed slot in advance; go on a clear day if you can — the stained glass is the show.
  • Food rhythm: Dinner after 8:30 pm is normal; use Explore · Barcelona for coffee, tapas, fish/veg, and spicier options.
  • Last night (Apr 7): If you need to be at BCN by ~7:55 AM (3h before a ~10:55 AM transatlantic), book a reliable taxi or stay near the airport — see Apr 8 section.

AVE hub

🌇 Madrid

  • Train: AVE Sants → Atocha — arrive ~20 min early; bags and snacks for a smooth ~2.5–3 h ride.
  • Neighborhoods: Retiro for a green reset; La Latina / Malasaña for tapas and evening energy.
  • Optional: Flamenco or a Ribera wine day — see Explore · Madrid and Logistics.
  • Football: Atlético vs Barcelona may fall on Sat Apr 4 in Madrid — huge, but collides with a same-day flight to Paris unless you delay. Times & picks: Logistics · Football.

Easter week

🥐 Paris

  • Apr 5 (Easter Sunday): Major sights may be open; shops often closed or short hours — check Google Maps the night before.
  • Museums: Book timed tickets for heavy hitters; mix in Marais walks and Seine time so the trip doesn’t feel like queue-craft.
  • CDG vs ORY: Pick flights that minimize schlep to your hotel; RER and Orlybus are fine with light luggage.
  • Apr 7 flight to BCN: Don’t schedule tight connections before the US-bound leg — you want a calm evening before Apr 8.
  • PSG at Parc des Princes: Best stadium fit for your nights in town — check home fixtures for Apr 5–6 and plan 5–7 h door-to-door. Match table & timing →
  • Seine dinner cruise: Eiffel-forward evening — Explore · Seine dinner cruise; bank steps by walking the quais or Trocadéro gardens earlier that day.

Transition day

✈️ Tuesday Apr 7 — Paris → Barcelona

  1. Morning: Light breakfast; finish packing; weigh checked bags if you’re near limits after shopping.
  2. Airport: Leave central Paris with buffer for RER/metro delays; international-ish stress is lower than US-bound but still allow comfort margin.
  3. BCN arrival: Aerobus or train to hotel — drop bags and keep the evening easy: walk, tapas, early-ish sleep.
  4. Night before BCN → ATL: Set two alarms; lay out passports, boarding pass, chargers, and empty water bottle to refill after security.

If you’re not staying at BCN the night of Apr 7

Published return times suggest a late-morning BCN departure — you still want a pre-booked ride and buffer for traffic. An airport hotel on Apr 7 removes cross-city risk entirely.

Return · Barcelona → Atlanta

🏠 Wednesday Apr 8 — Barcelona → Atlanta

Indicative times from FlightMapper (DL 195) for the BCN–ATL nonstop: depart ~10:55 AM Barcelona · arrive ~3:35 PM Atlanta same US calendar day (~10h40m block). Your ticket may show a different flight number (e.g. codeshare). Always verify in the Delta app and Delta flight status.

  1. Arrive early: If departure is ~10:55 AM, aim to be inside BCN by ~7:55 AM (3 hours). Add 30 min if checking bags or peak season.
  2. Check-in: Online as soon as it opens; if you need the desk (bags, document check), join the line with time to spare.
  3. Security: Liquids follow standard limits; laptops/electronics easy to access; wear shoes you can slip off if asked.
  4. After security: Refill water, grab a light meal — long-haul catering can run late; you’ll be happier boarded with a snack.
  5. Lounge (if eligible): SkyTeam / Delta status or Amex Platinum-type access can soften the wait — confirm which BCN lounge applies to your ticket same day.
  6. On board: Hydrate, stretch when allowed; set watch to Atlanta time and try to sleep toward the end if it’s a daytime eastbound.
  7. Atlanta: Immigration can take time — Global Entry / Mobile Passport help; allow connection buffer if you’re not terminating at ATL.

Minimal carry-on checklist

Passport · phone + charger · meds · headphones · layer for a cold cabin · pen for customs forms if still in use · copy of hotel address if asked.
