Foundation

📋 Before you go

  • Delta app: Save boarding passes offline; turn on flight notifications for DL 194 and DL 095.
  • Seats: For long hauls, aisle or window beats middle; if you sleep on planes, a window + neck pillow wins. Check seat maps for recline and proximity to galleys/lavatories.
  • Documents: Passport + a photo backup; ESTA isn’t needed for US citizens — others should verify entry rules.
  • Money: One no-foreign-fee card as primary; small cash (€) for tapas bars and taxis that don’t take cards.
  • Phone: eSIM or roaming plan so you have maps and Delta alerts in Spain and France.

Pro move

Pack a change of shirt + toothbrush in your carry-on. If a bag is delayed on the outbound or return, you’re still comfortable for day one or the ride home from ATL.

Start & finish

🌊 Barcelona

  • First days (Mar 31–Apr 1): Jet lag favors morning light outdoors — Gothic Quarter or waterfront walk before a late Spanish lunch.
  • Sagrada Família: Book a timed slot in advance; go on a clear day if you can — the stained glass is the show.
  • Food rhythm: Dinner after 8:30 pm is normal; use Explore · Barcelona for coffee and tapas anchors.
  • Last night (Apr 7): If DL 095 is early morning, consider staying near BCN or taking a pre-booked taxi — see Apr 8 section.

AVE hub

🌇 Madrid

  • Train: AVE Sants → Atocha — arrive ~20 min early; bags and snacks for a smooth ~2.5–3 h ride.
  • Neighborhoods: Retiro for a green reset; La Latina / Malasaña for tapas and evening energy.
  • Optional: Flamenco or a Ribera wine day — see Explore · Madrid and Logistics.

Easter week

🥐 Paris

  • Apr 5 (Easter Sunday): Major sights may be open; shops often closed or short hours — check Google Maps the night before.
  • Museums: Book timed tickets for heavy hitters; mix in Marais walks and Seine time so the trip doesn’t feel like queue-craft.
  • CDG vs ORY: Pick flights that minimize schlep to your hotel; RER and Orlybus are fine with light luggage.
  • Apr 7 flight to BCN: Don’t schedule tight connections before transatlantic night — you want calm before DL 095.

Transition day

✈️ Tuesday Apr 7 — Paris → Barcelona

  1. Morning: Light breakfast; finish packing; weigh checked bags if you’re near limits after shopping.
  2. Airport: Leave central Paris with buffer for RER/metro delays; international-ish stress is lower than US-bound but still allow comfort margin.
  3. BCN arrival: Aerobus or train to hotel — drop bags and keep the evening easy: walk, tapas, early-ish sleep.
  4. Night before DL 095: Set two alarms; lay out passports, boarding pass, chargers, and empty water bottle to refill after security.

If the flight is very early Apr 8

An airport hotel the night of Apr 7 removes city-to-BCN risk. If you stay in town, pre-book a reliable taxi or rideshare for a fixed wake-up time.

Return · Barcelona → Atlanta

🏠 Wednesday Apr 8 — Delta DL 095

BCN is your gateway home on DL 095. Times and terminal change — always verify same-day in the Delta app and Delta flight status.

  1. Arrive early: For US-bound flights from BCN, target 3 hours before departure (add 30 min if checking bags or during peak season).
  2. Check-in: Online as soon as it opens; if you need the desk (bags, document check), join the line with time to spare.
  3. Security: Liquids follow standard limits; laptops/electronics easy to access; wear shoes you can slip off if asked.
  4. After security: Refill water, grab a light meal — long-haul catering can run late; you’ll be happier boarded with a snack.
  5. Lounge (if eligible): SkyTeam / Delta status or Amex Platinum-type access can soften the wait — confirm which BCN lounge applies to your ticket same day.
  6. On board: Hydrate, stretch when allowed; set watch to Atlanta time and try to sleep toward the end if it’s a daytime eastbound.
  7. Atlanta: Immigration can take time — Global Entry / Mobile Passport help; allow connection buffer if you’re not terminating at ATL.

Minimal carry-on checklist

Passport · phone + charger · meds · headphones · layer for a cold cabin · pen for customs forms if still in use · copy of hotel address if asked.
